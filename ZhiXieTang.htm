Camling the Irritable Bowel

Zhi Xie Tang



Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS) is a group of digestive disorders consisting of abdominal cramps, bloating stomach and abnormal bowel movements such as diarrhea or constipation. Besides abnormal digestion, people may also experience anxiety, insomnia, and depression associated with the disorder. Chinese Medicine believe the IBS is related to stagnation of liver qi and an inharmonized spleen and liver. This imbalance leads to a disturbance in the mechanism of the digestive system. IBS is also related to an accumulation of the inner cold, resulting in weakness of the kidney yang and spleen yang. The stomach and spleen are not receiving the yang support from both the spleen and kidney, which leads to the abnormal function of the digestive system. Generally speaking, inappropriate diet, exhaustion, or extended coldness will worsen the symptoms.

Chinese Medicine outlines the following principles when dealing with IBS: regulate the liver qi, harmonize the balance between the liver and spleen, and support the kidney qi and yang. Yi Ji Zhi Xie Tang employs Mu Siang, Bai Shao, Chen Pi, and Fang Feng to balance the liver qi and harmonize with the spleen. Dang Shen, Bai Zhu, Fu Ling, and Gan Cao are used to tonify the spleen. Tian Xian Mao, Bu Gu Zhi, and Pao Jian are used to support the kidney yang. Dr. Zhong Da Jin, from First Peoles Hospital of Jiang Su Province, has treated 32 cases of IBS with this formula and received ver;y positive results. Through the use of Yi Ji Zhi Xie Tang, 24 of his patients recovered from IBS.

(The formula is best suited for IBS with diarrhea.)

Zhi Xie Tang is available both in granule and capsule form. Begin with 4.5 grams granule 2-3 times daily during the onset of symptoms. Once the condition stabilizes, it is recommended to use the capsule formula in a dose of 4 campsules twice a day for maintenance. A three month period of treatment is suggested, and the maintenance plan is optional dependingon the need of the patients.

Ingredients: Dang Shen, Chao Bai Zhu, Fu Ling, Zhi Gan Cao, Mu Xiang, Bai Shao, Chao Chen Pi, Fang Feng, Tian Xian Mao, Bu Gu Zhi, Pao Jiang.
